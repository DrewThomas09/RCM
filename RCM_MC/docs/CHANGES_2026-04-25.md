## TL;DR

Shipped **Gap J2 ‚Äî Regulatory-Arbitrage Collapse Detector** at the `/reg-arbitrage` route. This is the highest-leverage missing red-team module in the SeekingChartis gap matrix: it scores every corpus deal against five named regulatory arbitrages (NSA, 340B, V28, Medicaid MCO, ACO REACH) on a 0-100 fragility index, rolls the scores up via a weight-quadratic mean, and surfaces the deals stacking ‚â•3 high-fragility arbitrages as the **Steward / Envision / CARD pattern**. The module synthesizes five existing data_public modules (`nsa_idr_modeler`, `tracker_340b`, `risk_adjustment`, `medicaid_unwinding`, `aco_economics`) into a unified collapse index. It maps directly to two prompt-library prompts: P02 (quarterly market-structure scan) and P16 (failed-deal pre-mortem).

### 1. `rcm_mc/data_public/regulatory_arbitrage_collapse.py` (+612)

**Public API:**

| Symbol | Purpose |
|---|---|

**Calibration notes:**

- Severity bands: `critical` ‚â• 75, `high` ‚â• 55, `medium` ‚â• 30, else `low`
- Aggregate index uses a weight-quadratic mean so a single 90 dominates five 30s ‚Äî that's the Steward fingerprint we're surfacing
- Steward-pattern flag triggers when ‚â•3 arbitrages reach `high` severity
- Pre-mortem recommendation: `STOP` at composite ‚â•70, `PROCEED_WITH_CONDITIONS` at 55-70, else `PROCEED`

**Word-boundary keyword matcher.** The first calibration pass produced a runaway false-positive: 467/745 deals scored at AC-1 high+ because the NSA-affected-specialty list included `"ED"` and `"ER"` and substring-matched into "covered", "issued", "raised", "Premier", "shareholders", "buyer". Fix: `_has_keyword(haystack, needles)` switches behavior on token length ‚Äî tokens ‚â§ 4 chars use a word-boundary regex `(?<![a-z0-9])<token>(?![a-z0-9])`; longer tokens substring-match. After the fix, the AC-1 critical+high count dropped to 79, top deal is correctly MEDNAX Radiology ‚Äî exactly the kind of hospital-based physician footprint NSA targets.
