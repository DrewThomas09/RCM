# Partnerships Strategy + Outreach Plan

The platform's value compounds with partners — every additional
data source, every distribution channel, every advisor with the
platform on their workstation accelerates the next 100 customers.
This document maps the partnership landscape, ranks opportunities
by leverage-per-effort, and lays out concrete outreach plans for
the top tier.

## Partnership categories + their value

Five partnership types, each with a different value mechanic:

  1. **Data providers** — they have the feeds; we have the
     analytics layer. Pass-through licensing or white-label
     embedded.
  2. **PE operating firms** — boutique healthcare-PE operating
     consultancies who add post-close value. They could resell
     the platform alongside their services.
  3. **Healthcare consultancies** — Big 4 + boutique advisory
     firms doing diligence for PE clients. Embedded
     analytical layer.
  4. **Channel partners** — PE-focused law firms, placement
     agents, fund admins. Refer clients into our funnel.
  5. **Industry organizations** — HFMA, AHA, ILPA, academic
     centers. Thought leadership + credibility.

Different partnership types optimize for different growth levers:

  - **Customer acquisition velocity** → channel partners +
    consultancies
  - **Product depth** → data providers + industry organizations
  - **Brand defensibility** → academic + ILPA + HFMA
  - **Competitive moat** → exclusive or quasi-exclusive data
    relationships

The plan below targets all four; resource allocation per quarter
shifts based on which lever the GTM team is pulling hardest.

## Tier-1 priority partnerships (engage in next 6 months)

### Definitive Healthcare — premium data feed pass-through

**The fit**: Definitive's provider-affiliation database is the
most-cited healthcare data feed in PE healthcare. Customers
already license it; bringing those licenses through our
platform's Enterprise tier (per the data acquisition strategy)
makes the platform stickier without us paying for the data.

**The ask**:
- Mutual referral agreement: their sales team flags PE
  customers; we flag Definitive when our customers ask for
  affiliation data.
- API integration: Definitive's REST API surfaces inside our
  comparable_finder + asset profile pages. Customer with their
  own license sees their data populated automatically.
- Co-marketing: joint webinar 'Healthcare PE Diligence with
  Definitive Data + the Platform's Analytical Layer'.

**The pitch**:
> "You're a data company. Healthcare PE customers license your
> feeds and then build their own dashboards in Tableau. They're
> doing analytical work badly. We're a software platform whose
> analytics layer slots directly on top of your data — your
> customers get more value from your feed, you stay sticky to
> them. Bilateral referral on customers we both serve."

**Outreach plan**:
- Week 1: warm-intro through a mutual customer or Bain
  Capital Ventures (a Definitive investor) network.
- Week 3: discovery call with their head of partnerships +
  CTO.
- Week 8: API-integration MVP (read-only Definitive data
  pulled into our compares).
- Week 12: joint sales motion test on 2-3 named customers.
- Quarter 2: formalize co-marketing + revenue share.

### Trilliant Health — alternate to Definitive

**The fit**: Same as Definitive but more healthcare-PE-specific
positioning. They're hungry for distribution; we're hungry for
data partnership credibility.

**The ask**: Same shape as Definitive — bilateral referral +
API + co-marketing. We probably can't do exclusive deals with
both; pick whichever signs faster + has cleaner sales motion.

**Outreach plan**: Identical to Definitive; run in parallel.
First-to-sign wins primary partnership; second moves to
secondary.

### Houlihan Lokey + Triple Tree (healthcare M&A advisory)

**The fit**: These banks advise on ~30-40% of mid-market
healthcare PE deals. They build CIMs (the documents the
platform's CIM extraction pipeline targets — per the
next-cycle plan). Banker pass-through is in our pricing model
($5-15K per mandate — per the business model plan).

**The ask**:
- Banker pass-through licensing: when they're the sell-side
  advisor, the buyer side automatically gets a free 60-day
  license to do diligence on the platform.
- Embedded analytics in their CIM workflow: they paste deal
  data into our system; we generate per-deal one-pagers they
  can include in CIM packages.
- Banker training program: train their associates on how to
  use the platform (10-day onboarding).

**The pitch**:
> "Your CIMs are the entry point to every PE buyer. The buyer
> spends 6-8 hours typing your CIM into a spreadsheet. We
> auto-extract that work in 30 minutes. If you bake our 60-day
> pass into the CIM package, your buyers get analytical depth
> immediately — they're more likely to bid, faster. You're a
> hero to the seller; they're a hero to their IC."

**Outreach plan**:
- Week 1: warm-intro through Houlihan or Triple Tree senior
  banker (reference: prior PE customers).
- Week 4: discovery + commercial framework discussion.
- Week 8: pilot on 1-2 live mandates (free for them; data
  used to refine the CIM extraction pipeline).
- Quarter 2: formalize banker-pass-through pricing.

### HFMA (Healthcare Financial Management Association)

**The fit**: HFMA is the standards body for healthcare RCM. The
platform's predictors + EBITDA bridge are calibrated against
HFMA MAP Keys; partnering for HFMA-endorsed methodology gives
us defensibility credibility no competitor can replicate
quickly.

**The ask**:
- Co-author white paper: 'Healthcare RCM Predictability —
  Calibrating PE Diligence Against HFMA MAP Keys'. Join HFMA's
  research advisory group.
- Sponsor HFMA's annual conference; speak at the analytics
  track.
- HFMA-endorsed certification: our predictors carry an 'HFMA
  MAP-aligned' badge in the UI (per the metric_glossary
  module).

**The pitch**:
> "Your MAP Keys are the de-facto RCM benchmark. Our platform
> calibrates against them; partner shops cite our predictions
> in IC. Joint research positions HFMA as the methodology
> authority and the platform as the analytical implementation.
> Win-win for credibility and for member-firm value."

**Outreach plan**:
- Week 1: contact HFMA Research VP + analytics committee chair
  via LinkedIn.
- Week 4: pitch the white-paper concept.
- Week 12: white paper drafted; submit for HFMA review.
- Quarter 2: publication + conference sponsorship + UI badge.

### CMS Innovation Center direct engagement

**The fit**: CMMI publishes new APMs (LEAD model per the
regulatory roadmap, GUIDE model, etc.) on rolling basis. Direct
relationships give us advance visibility on rule changes; we
become first-to-market on the analytical layer for each new
model.

**The ask**:
- Apply to be an authorized CMMI Research Partner (their
  formal program).
- Attend public CMMI listening sessions; submit written
  comments on proposed rules.
- Direct relationships with the CMMI program leads for the
  models that affect PE assets most (LEAD, GUIDE, ACO REACH,
  Direct Contracting if reincarnated).

**The pitch**:
> "PE-owned healthcare entities represent ~15% of CMS-paid
> volume. They're disproportionately affected by your APM
> models. Our platform gives investors transparency into APM
> economics. Joint research with CMMI on PE-owned-asset
> performance under your models — both sides win on data
> quality."

**Outreach plan**:
- Week 1: written outreach to CMMI Director's office +
  program leads.
- Week 8: in-person Washington DC visit; take 2-3 meetings
  per visit.
- Quarter 2: research-partner status; joint white paper
  proposal.

## Tier-2 partnerships (engage Q3-Q4 of next year)

### Big 4 healthcare consulting practices (Deloitte / EY / KPMG / PwC)

**The fit**: Big 4 PE diligence practices serve every
mega-fund. They charge $500K-2M per diligence. Our platform
embedded in their workflow makes them faster + more
defensible. Risk: they're competitors-as-much-as-partners.

**The ask**: Embedded software partnership. Big 4 consultant
runs the platform during a diligence; client receives the
output as part of the consultant's deliverable. Big 4 white-
labels parts of the analysis.

**Outreach plan**: Cold outreach is hard at Big 4 size.
Warm-intro through a mutual customer or Big 4 alumnus working
at our customer. Q3 timing once we have the customer
references to support the pitch.

### Boutique healthcare consultancies (Health Advances, ECG, Berkeley Research Group)

**The fit**: Boutique strategy consultants (50-300 person
firms) doing healthcare PE work. They value the platform's
analytical depth; they don't have the engineering muscle to
build similar tools.

**The ask**: Embedded license at the consultancy level
($150K/yr for 5 seats); they pass cost through on engagements.
Adjunct: consultancies refer their clients to us when the
client wants a long-term in-house tool rather than a one-off
engagement.

**Outreach plan**: Q3 Q4 outreach via warm intro from current
or future PE customers who already use these consultancies.

### Cloud marketplace partnerships (AWS Marketplace, Azure Marketplace)

**The fit**: Customers procuring through cloud marketplace can
spend committed cloud budgets. Marketplace presence reduces
sales friction by ~30-50% for cloud-procurement-disposed shops.

**The ask**: List on AWS Marketplace + Azure Marketplace as a
BYOL (Bring Your Own License) software offering. AWS handles
billing + procurement; we handle delivery.

**Outreach plan**: Q3 — relatively low effort (well-documented
process). Pick AWS first (larger PE-customer footprint); Azure
6-12 months later.

## Tier-3 partnerships (engage opportunistically)

**Outreach**: Q4 — needs the ILPA 2.0 module fully built first

## Tier-4 partnerships (defer or decline)

### Mega-consultancies (McKinsey, BCG, Bain)

**The fit**: They're far too big to partner with effectively.
Their healthcare PE practices have 100+ consultants; their
internal tooling is whatever they want to build.

**The decision**: Don't pursue. Position them as adjacent —
they reference us in their reports if we earn it through
brand. Don't try to formalize.

### Healthcare RCM software vendors (Cedar, Olive AI, Notable)

**The fit**: Adjacent — their tool runs *inside* portfolio
companies; ours runs *over* the portfolio. Possible
post-close-monitoring integration but not core to PE-buyer
workflow.

**The decision**: Defer to year 3+. Plumb their data into our
post-close monitoring as it matures.

## Outreach playbook

For each Tier-1 partnership, the outreach mechanic is consistent:

  1. **Warm-intro first.** Cold outreach to senior partnerships
     leaders has <5% reply rate. A warm intro from a mutual
     customer or board-overlap contact has >50%. Spend 2-3
     weeks identifying the right warm path before sending the
     first email.
  2. **First meeting agenda**: 30 minutes — 10 min discovery
     of their priorities + 15 min our pitch + 5 min next-step.
     Don't try to close in the first meeting.
  3. **Follow-up cadence**: weekly until pilot signed; biweekly
     once piloting; monthly once formalized.
  4. **Pilot before paper**: every Tier-1 partnership starts
     with a 60-day pilot at no commercial commitment. The
     pilot tests fit; the contract follows the pilot's
     learning.
  5. **Joint goals doc**: every formalized partnership has a
     written 6-month goals doc — what each party delivers,
     what success looks like, how we measure.
  6. **Quarterly review**: every Tier-1 partner gets a
     quarterly business review covering joint pipeline,
     mutual referrals, integration metrics, customer
     feedback.

## Resource allocation

The partnership work is real headcount. Recommended:

  - **Year 1**: 1 FTE Head of Partnerships + occasional
    executive support (CEO + CTO for high-stakes meetings).
    Focus: Tier-1 + Tier-2.
  - **Year 2**: +1 FTE for partnership operations (integration
    delivery, joint marketing execution). Tier-3 starts.
  - **Year 3**: +1 FTE for academic + industry-org relations.
    Full Tier-4 evaluation.

## Risk + mitigation

  - **Risk: Definitive or Trilliant declines partnership and
    pivots to compete.** Mitigation: don't disclose our full
    roadmap in early conversations; protect IP; build
    customer-licensed-pass-through architecture (per data
    acquisition plan) so we're not single-vendor-dependent.

  - **Risk: Big 4 partnership turns into Big 4 building a
    competing tool.** Mitigation: don't share architectural
    details + don't enable enough learning that they could
    replicate without engineering investment. Keep them
    embedded but not deeply integrated.

  - **Risk: Customer reactions to partnerships ('they own all
    your data now').** Mitigation: every partnership preserves
    the customer's data sovereignty. Customer's data flows
    into our platform; partnership flow is metadata + business
    referrals, never customer data.

  - **Risk: Partnership obligations slow product velocity.**
    Mitigation: partnership commitments capped — no more than
    20% of any quarter's engineering capacity goes to
    partner-required features. Partners that demand more
    don't get formalized.

## Success metrics

  - **Year 1**: 2 Tier-1 partnerships formalized (data provider
    + advisor). 1 Tier-2 (Big 4) in pilot. 30% of new customer
    acquisition coming through partnerships by Q4.
  - **Year 2**: 4 Tier-1 partnerships formalized; 2 Tier-2;
    50% of new customer acquisition through partnerships;
    HFMA-endorsed methodology badge live in UI.
  - **Year 3**: full ecosystem — Big 4 + boutique
    consultancies + cloud marketplaces + advisor channel +
    academic citations. 60%+ of new customer acquisition
    through partnerships.

## What we don't try to be

  - **A partner network for partners' sake**. Every partnership
    serves a specific lever (acquisition, depth,
    defensibility, moat). We refuse partnerships that don't
    move one of those four.
  - **A reseller of someone else's platform**. We're a
    software platform first; partners enrich our platform but
    don't replace it.
  - **A consultancy ourselves**. We have customers + we have
    partners; we don't have a services arm. Boutique
    consultancies are our partners, not our competition or
    our internal capability.

The line: partnerships make the platform more valuable to
customers and harder for competitors to replicate. Anything
that doesn't do both isn't a partnership we sign.

## Bottom line

Partnerships in this market are a force multiplier, not a
replacement for direct sales. Year 1 should target two
high-leverage Tier-1 relationships (Definitive *or* Trilliant +
HFMA) plus a banker channel (Houlihan or Triple Tree) — three
deals total, signed in 6-month sequence. Years 2-3 layer in Big
4, boutique consultancies, cloud marketplaces, and academic
relations.

The compounding effect: by Year 3, the platform isn't just one
software vendor selling to PE shops. It's the analytical layer
endorsed by HFMA, integrated with the leading data feeds,
sold through the major M&A bankers, and cited in academic
healthcare-PE research. Every layer makes displacement harder.

That's the partnership-driven moat. Build it deliberately —
two partnerships per quarter at most — but never stop.
