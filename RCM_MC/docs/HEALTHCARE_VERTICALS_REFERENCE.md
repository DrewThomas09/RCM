# US Healthcare Verticals: A Granular Operational, Clinical & Epidemiological Reference (PEDesk/RCM-MC)

**Scope note:** This is an operational/clinical/epidemiological reference, not an investment document. Each vertical is profiled across: (a) common billing/clinical codes, (b) patient population & epidemiology, (c) provider/facility counts & workforce, (c2) geographic access & distance, (d) operational/clinical benchmarks, (e) reimbursement/payer context, (f) key data sources. Codes are representative high-frequency examples drawn from CPT/HCPCS, ICD-10-CM, MS-DRG, APC, CDT, and NUCC provider taxonomy systems; specific facility/provider counts and epidemiology are sourced to CMS, CDC, HRSA, AAMC, SEER, USRDS, SAMHSA, ADA, BLS, MedPAC and peer-reviewed literature. Figures vary by source vintage; conflicts are flagged.

## TL;DR

- The US physician workforce numbered **1,032,365 active physicians in 2024** (866,460 in direct patient care; 304 and 255 per 100,000 respectively, AAMC), and care is increasingly ambulatory — there are ~6,400–6,500 Medicare-certified ASCs, ~11,900–14,000 urgent care centers, 7,556 dialysis centers, ~14,742 nursing facilities, and ~1,400 FQHC organizations running 16,200+ sites.
- Each vertical has a characteristic code "fingerprint" (e.g., GI = colonoscopy CPT 45378/45380/45385 + screening G0121; ophthalmology = cataract 66984/66982; dialysis = ESRD PPS bundle 90935/90937 + MCP 90951–90970; SNF = PDPM HIPPS on revenue code 0022), a distinct epidemiology, and a governing payment system (PFS, OPPS, ASC, IPPS/MS-DRG, IRF-PPS, LTCH-PPS, PDGM, PDPM, ESRD PPS, IPF-PPS, CLFS, or CDT/dental).
- Access is highly uneven: ~20% of Americans live in a primary-care HPSA, nearly 60 million live in dental-shortage areas (HRSA, March 2024), rural residents travel on average 10.5 miles/17 minutes to the nearest hospital vs 4.4 miles/10 minutes urban (Pew), and ED patients travel a national average of 8.0 miles/17.3 minutes (BMC Health Services Research, 2022).

## Key Findings

- **Workforce concentration:** Primary care (internal medicine, family medicine, pediatrics) holds the largest active-physician counts; surgical specialties (esp. orthopedics, neurosurgery) and psychiatry face the most acute shortages and maldistribution.
- **Site-of-care migration:** Per ASC Data's Industry Overview (Feb 2025), "more than 80% of surgeries are now performed in an outpatient setting"; ASCs concentrate in GI, ophthalmology, orthopedics, and increasingly cardiology/pain.
- **Chronic disease drives volume:** 40.1M Americans (12.0%) have diagnosed or undiagnosed diabetes (CDC National Diabetes Statistics Report); ~37M (14%) have CKD (≈87–90% unaware); >808,000 live with ESKD; ~2,001,140 new cancer cases projected for 2024 (ACS/SEER).
- **Behavioral health gap:** 61.5M adults (23.4%) have Any Mental Illness and 14.6M (5.6%) Serious Mental Illness (SAMHSA 2024 NSDUH); of the 52.6M people who needed substance-use treatment in 2024, only 3.5% (≈10.2M) received it — meaning roughly 80% went untreated.
- **Payment-system diversity** is the central operational reality: each facility vertical answers to a different prospective payment system with distinct assessment instruments (OASIS, MDS 3.0, IRF-PAI) and quality programs.

## Details by Vertical

### PHYSICIAN SPECIALTIES / PRACTICE TYPES

#### Primary Care / Family Medicine & Internal Medicine

(a) **Codes:** E/M office visits CPT 99202–99215 (new/established); annual wellness visits G0438/G0439; preventive 99381–99397; ICD-10 high-frequency: I10 (essential hypertension), E11.9 (type 2 diabetes), E78.5 (hyperlipidemia), Z00.00 (general adult exam), J06.9 (URI), M54.50 (low back pain). Taxonomy: 207Q00000X (Family Medicine), 207R00000X (Internal Medicine). POS 11 (office), 02/10 (telehealth).
(b) **Epidemiology:** PCPs are first contact for the bulk of chronic disease: hypertension affects roughly 120M+ US adults; diabetes 40.1M (12.0%, CDC); hyperlipidemia tens of millions. In 2023, 340,319 primary care physicians were actively working (34.3% of all US physicians, HRSA), plus an estimated 374,970 NPs.
(c) **Workforce:** Primary care is the largest physician block; 35%+ of PCPs are age 55+. Rural areas have markedly lower PCP-to-population ratios.
(c2) **Access:** About 20% of the US population lives in a primary-care HPSA (HRSA). Rural-urban gradient in PCP ratios is well documented (USDA ERS, HRSA).
(d) **Benchmarks:** Panel sizes ~1,800–2,500 (often cited ~2,300); 18–25 visits/day typical; no-show rates ~5–30% by setting; HEDIS measures (Controlling High Blood Pressure, HbA1c control, breast/colorectal screening) and CMS Star ratings.
(e) **Reimbursement:** Medicare Physician Fee Schedule (PFS); mixed commercial/Medicare/Medicaid; value-based contracts (MSSP ACOs, PCMH).
(f) **Sources:** HRSA State of the Primary Care Workforce 2025; AAMC Physician Workforce Dashboard; CDC National Diabetes Statistics Report; NCQA HEDIS.

#### Pediatrics

(a) Well-child 99381–99395, vaccine admin 90460–90461 + product CPT; ICD-10 Z00.121/Z00.129 (well-child exam w/wo abnormal findings), J06.9, H66.90 (otitis media), J45.909 (asthma). Taxonomy 208000000X. POS 11.
(b) Pediatric population = US under-18 (~73M). Pediatrics has the highest female-physician share (66.7% in 2024, AAMC).
(c) Among the largest specialties; ratios computed against population under 24/18.
(d) Vaccination rates (HEDIS Childhood Immunization Status Combo 10), well-child visit rates, developmental screening.
(e) Medicaid/CHIP is a dominant payer for pediatrics (often 40%+ of volume).
(f) CDC NIS (immunizations); AAP; HEDIS.

#### Dermatology

(a) Skin biopsy CPT 11102–11107; destruction of premalignant lesions 17000/17003/17004; excision of malignant lesions 11600-series; Mohs 17311–17315; pathology 88305. ICD-10: L40.0 (psoriasis), C44.x (skin cancers), L70.0 (acne), L57.0 (actinic keratosis). Taxonomy 207N00000X.
(b) Skin cancer is the most common US cancer; >5M nonmelanoma cases treated annually (excluded from SEER incidence counts); melanoma ~100,000 new cases/year.
(c) Supply-constrained relative to demand; long wait times.
(d) High procedure throughput; Mohs cure rates ~97–99% for primary BCC; visit volumes among the highest of medical specialties.
(e) PFS + ASC/OPPS for surgical; significant cosmetic cash-pay component.
(f) SEER; American Academy of Dermatology; AAMC.

#### Ophthalmology & Optometry

(a) **Ophthalmology:** cataract extraction with IOL CPT 66984 (routine) / 66982 (complex); intravitreal injection 67028; YAG capsulotomy 66821; OCT 92134; comprehensive eye exam 92004/92014. ICD-10 H25.x (age-related cataract), H35.31 (dry AMD), H35.32 (wet AMD), H40.x (glaucoma), E11.3x (diabetic retinopathy). Taxonomy 207W00000X. **Optometry:** 92002–92014, refraction 92015; taxonomy 152W00000X.
(b) Cataract is the highest-volume operation in Medicare; AMD and diabetic retinopathy rising with aging/diabetes; ~24M+ Americans have cataract.
(c) Optometrists: 47,930 (BLS OEWS). Ophthalmologists far fewer; primary eye care often via optometry.
(d) Cataract is a flagship ASC procedure; case times ~10–20 min skin-to-skin in high-volume centers; complication rates low.
(e) Cataract paid under ASC/OPPS (facility) + PFS (surgeon); anti-VEGF injections a major Part B drug spend.
(f) AAO IRIS Registry; Medicare Part B; NEI.

#### Gastroenterology

(a) Colonoscopy CPT 45378 (diagnostic), 45380 (with biopsy), 45385 (with snare polypectomy); screening HCPCS G0105 (high risk)/G0121 (average risk); upper endoscopy 43235/43239; ICD-10 Z12.11 (screening colon CA), K21.9 (GERD), K57.30 (diverticulosis), K50/K51 (IBD). Taxonomy 207RG0100X.
(b) Colorectal cancer screening recommended ages 45–75; colonoscopy among the highest-volume outpatient procedures (millions/year). CRC ~150,000 new cases/year (SEER).
(c) High-demand specialty; endoscopy heavily ASC-based.
(d) Key quality metrics: adenoma detection rate (ADR; benchmark ≥25% overall, ≥30% men/≥20% women), cecal intubation rate ≥90–95%, withdrawal time ≥6 min, bowel-prep adequacy.
(e) ASC/OPPS facility + PFS; commercial + Medicare.
(f) GIQuIC registry; SEER; USPSTF.

#### Orthopedic Surgery

(a) Total knee arthroplasty CPT 27447; total hip 27130; knee arthroscopy 29881; rotator cuff repair 23412; spinal fusion 22630/22633. MS-DRG 469/470 (major joint replacement lower extremity w/wo MCC). ICD-10 M17.x (knee OA), M16.x (hip OA), S-codes (fractures). Taxonomy 207X00000X.
(b) Osteoarthritis affects ~32M+ US adults; >1M joint replacements/year; volumes growing with aging.
(c) Among the lowest female-physician shares (6.8% in 2024, AAMC).
(d) Major-joint cases migrating to ASCs; inpatient TKA ALOS dropping; readmission and SSI rates tracked under CMS programs (CJR/bundled-payment history).
(e) MS-DRG 469/470 (inpatient IPPS), increasingly ASC/OPPS outpatient; PFS surgeon fee.
(f) AJRR (American Joint Replacement Registry); CMS; AAOS.

#### Cardiology

(a) Diagnostic cath CPT 93458; PCI with stent 92928; echocardiogram 93306; pacemaker insertion 33208; cardiac MRI 75561. MS-DRG 246–251 (PCI), 222–227 (cardiac defib). ICD-10 I25.10 (CAD), I48.x (atrial fibrillation), I50.x (heart failure), I21.x (MI). Taxonomy 207RC0000X.
(b) Cardiovascular disease is the leading US cause of death; HF ~6M+ adults; AFib millions; CAD highly prevalent. Interventional cardiology is a growing subspecialty.
(c) High-demand; procedures shifting toward ASC settings.
(d) Door-to-balloon time (STEMI; goal ≤90 min), 30-day AMI/HF readmission and mortality (CMS), cath-lab utilization. **Projection (not realized):** per ResearchAndMarkets' 2024 US ASC Market Report, "By 2025, 33% of cardiology procedures are projected to be performed in ASCs" (originally a Bain & Co. projection).
(e) Mix of IPPS (MS-DRG), OPPS, ASC, PFS.
(f) ACC NCDR registries; CMS; AHA.

#### Urology

(a) Cystoscopy CPT 52000; TURP 52601; prostate biopsy 55700; lithotripsy 50590; vasectomy 55250. ICD-10 N40.x (BPH), C61 (prostate CA), N20.0 (kidney stone), R33 (retention). Taxonomy 208800000X.
(b) BPH affects a majority of older men; prostate cancer ~290,000 new cases/year (most common male cancer); kidney stones ~10% lifetime prevalence.
(c) Workforce shortage and aging; rural access limited.
(d) ASC-suitable procedures common; PSA screening metrics.
(e) ASC/OPPS + PFS; Medicare-heavy due to age skew.
(f) AUA; SEER; Medicare.

#### Otolaryngology (ENT)

(a) Tonsillectomy CPT 42826; tympanostomy 69436; functional endoscopic sinus surgery 31256/31267; nasal endoscopy 31231. ICD-10 J35.x (chronic tonsil/adenoid), J32.x (chronic sinusitis), H65/H66 (otitis media), R49.0 (dysphonia). Taxonomy 207Y00000X.
(b) Chronic sinusitis ~11–12% of adults; otitis media a leading pediatric diagnosis; OSA growing.
(c) ENT procedures heavily ASC-based.
(d) Tympanostomy and tonsillectomy among most common pediatric surgeries.
(e) ASC/OPPS + PFS.
(f) AAO-HNS; CDC.

#### Neurology

(a) EEG CPT 95816/95819; EMG/NCS 95910-series; Botox for migraine 64615; lumbar puncture 62270. ICD-10 G40.x (epilepsy), G20 (Parkinson), G35 (MS), R51 (headache), G30.x (Alzheimer), I63.x (stroke sequelae). Taxonomy 2084N0400X.
(b) Migraine ~39M Americans; epilepsy ~3.4M; Parkinson ~1M; MS ~1M; Alzheimer ~6.7M. Aging drives demand.
(c) Shortage acute; long wait times for new patients.
(d) Door-to-needle (stroke tPA ≤60 min); telestroke expanding access.
(e) PFS predominant; infusibles a growing Part B cost.
(f) AAN; CDC; Alzheimer's Association.

#### Oncology / Hematology

(a) Chemotherapy admin CPT 96413/96415/96417; drug J-codes (e.g., J9355 trastuzumab); bone-marrow biopsy 38221. ICD-10 C-codes by site, D-codes (D64.9 anemia, D69.6 thrombocytopenia), Z51.11 (chemo encounter). Taxonomy 207RH0000X, 2055H0100X.
(b) ~2,001,140 new cancer cases and 611,720 deaths projected in 2024 (ACS/SEER); >18M US cancer survivors. Cancer mortality has declined, averting >4M deaths since 1991.
(c) Medical oncology delivered largely in office/infusion settings and HOPDs.
(d) Infusion-chair utilization, time-to-treatment-initiation, OCM/EOM quality measures, regimen adherence.
(e) Part B drugs (buy-and-bill), PFS, OPPS; 340B relevant to hospital-based oncology.
(f) SEER; ACS Cancer Facts & Figures; CMS OCM/EOM.

#### Rheumatology, Endocrinology, Pulmonology (medical subspecialties)

(a) **Rheum:** infusion 96365/96413, joint injection 20610; ICD-10 M05/M06 (RA), M32 (lupus), M10 (gout). Taxonomy 207RR0500X. **Endo:** CGM 95251, thyroid US 76536; E11.x, E05 (hyperthyroid), E03 (hypothyroid), E66 (obesity). 207RE0101X. **Pulm:** spirometry 94010, bronchoscopy 31622, polysomnography 95810; J44 (COPD), J45 (asthma), J84 (ILD), G47.33 (OSA). 207RP1001X.
(b) RA ~1.3M; gout ~9M; COPD ~15M diagnosed; asthma ~25M; OSA tens of millions; diabetes 40.1M with endocrinology managing complex cases. Pulmonary disease has a notably older workforce (73.6% age 65+ in 2024, reflecting reclassification toward pulmonary critical care).
(c) All three are shortage specialties; endocrinology especially undersupplied for the diabetes burden.
(d) Biologic-infusion utilization; HbA1c control (HEDIS); spirometry-confirmed COPD.
(e) PFS + Part B biologics; infusion suites.
(f) ACR, Endocrine Society, ATS registries; CDC.

#### Nephrology

(a) Dialysis MCP CPT 90951–90970 (monthly capitated by age/visit count); AV fistula creation 36821; ICD-10 N18.1–N18.6 (CKD stages; N18.6 = ESRD), Z99.2 (dialysis dependence). Taxonomy 207RN0300X.
(b) ~37M US adults (14%) have CKD; ~87% of those with CKD are unaware; >808,000 with ESKD (68% dialysis, 32% transplant); diabetes and hypertension cause ~75% of incident ESRD. Black Americans are 4× more likely to develop ESKD (NIDDK/USRDS).
(c) Nephrologists manage CKD/ESRD across 7,556 dialysis centers (see below).
(d) Kt/V adequacy ≥1.2, vascular access (fistula-first), hospitalization rate, mortality (187.7 per 1,000 person-years on dialysis vs 74.3 with transplant, NIDDK).
(e) ESRD PPS bundle + MCP; near-universal Medicare ESRD entitlement.
(f) USRDS Annual Data Report; CDC CKD Surveillance; NIDDK.

#### OB-GYN / Women's Health & Fertility/REI

(a) **OB-GYN:** vaginal delivery CPT 59400 (global), cesarean 59510; laparoscopic hysterectomy 58571; Pap 88175; ICD-10 O80 (normal delivery), Z34.x (supervision of pregnancy), D25.x (fibroids). MS-DRG 766–768, 783–788 (deliveries). Taxonomy 207V00000X. **REI/Fertility:** oocyte retrieval 58970, embryo transfer 58974/58976; ICD-10 N97.x (infertility). 207VE0102X.
(b) ~3.6M US births/year; OB-GYN has a 64.1% female workforce (AAMC). Infertility affects ~1 in 8 couples; IVF cycles >400,000/year (SART). HRSA Maternity Care Target Areas (MCTAs) flag shortage.
(c) OB-GYN shortage in rural areas; many counties are "maternity care deserts."
(d) Cesarean rate (NTSV C-section benchmark ~23.6%), severe maternal morbidity, IVF live-birth rate per transfer.
(e) Deliveries IPPS/MS-DRG (facility); Medicaid pays ~40% of US births; fertility largely cash/commercial.
(f) CDC WONDER/Natality; SART/CDC ART; HRSA.

#### Pain Management & Anesthesiology

(a) **Pain:** epidural steroid injection CPT 62323, facet injection 64493, RFA 64635; ICD-10 M54.5, M48.06, G89.29. Taxonomy 208VP0014X. **Anesthesia:** CPT 00100–01999 (base + time units), modifiers AA/QK/QX/QZ; taxonomy 207L00000X.
(b) Chronic pain affects ~50M+ US adults; high ASC procedure volume.
(c) Anesthesiology faces shortage; CRNAs supplement (taxonomy 367500000X).
(d) Anesthesia billed in time units; pain injections ASC-suitable; PACU turnover.
(e) Anesthesia conversion-factor based; ASC/OPPS for pain procedures.
(f) ASA; CMS.

#### Radiology & Pathology

(a) **Radiology:** CT abdomen/pelvis w/contrast CPT 74177; MRI brain 70553; CXR 71046; screening mammography 77067; professional/technical split modifiers 26/TC. Taxonomy 2085R0202X. APCs govern OPPS imaging. **Pathology:** 88305 (tissue exam, most common), 88307/88309; clinical lab 80048/80053, 85025; taxonomy 207ZP0102X. CLFS governs clinical lab.
(b) Imaging volumes very high; mammography screening for women 40–74.
(c) Radiology workforce growth lagging demand; teleradiology expanding access. Pathologist supply redefined by subspecialization (CAP/AAMC).
(d) Report turnaround time, critical-result communication, peer-review concordance; lab TAT.
(e) Imaging: PFS (freestanding/MPFS) vs OPPS (HOPD); pathology CPT + CLFS for clinical lab.
(f) ACR; CAP; CMS.

#### General Surgery & Plastic Surgery

(a) **General:** lap cholecystectomy CPT 47562; lap appendectomy 44970; inguinal hernia repair 49505/49650; ICD-10 K80.x, K35.x, K40.x. MS-DRG 338–343 (appendectomy), 411–419. Taxonomy 208600000X. **Plastic:** breast reconstruction 19357, blepharoplasty 15823; 208200000X; large cosmetic cash-pay.
(b) General surgery supply grew only ~3% 2018–2022 vs 5.4% all specialties (AAMC/ACS); projected shortfall. DC has the highest general-surgeon density (24.1/100,000); rural maldistribution stark.
(c) ~152,700 surgeons across all surgical specialties (AAMC 2019 baseline); **projection (not realized):** AAMC forecasts a shortage of 10,000–19,900 surgeons by 2036.
(d) SSI rates, NSQIP risk-adjusted outcomes, ALOS.
(e) IPPS/OPPS/ASC + PFS; cosmetic cash.
(f) ACS NSQIP; AAMC; CMS.

#### Psychiatry

(a) Psychiatric diagnostic eval CPT 90792; psychotherapy 90832/90834/90837; E/M + add-on psychotherapy; ICD-10 F32/F33 (depression), F41 (anxiety), F20 (schizophrenia), F31 (bipolar), F90 (ADHD). MS-DRG 880–887. Taxonomy 2084P0800X.
(b) **61.5M adults (23.4%) have Any Mental Illness; 14.6M (5.6%) Serious Mental Illness** in 2024 (SAMHSA NSDUH). 21.4M adults had a past-year major depressive episode; ~48% of adults with AMI received no treatment.
(c) Among the most shortage-prone specialties; mental-health HPSAs widespread (geographic designation threshold 30,000:1 population-to-psychiatrist).
(d) Follow-up after hospitalization for mental illness (HEDIS FUH 7/30-day), high no-show rates, telepsychiatry adoption.
(e) PFS; parity laws; Medicaid a major payer for SMI.
(f) SAMHSA NSDUH; HRSA HPSA; HEDIS.

#### Dentistry & Dental Specialties

(a) **CDT codes:** D0120 (periodic exam), D0150 (comprehensive), D0210/D0274 (radiographs), D1110 (adult prophylaxis), D2391/D2392 (composite restorations), D2740 (crown), D7140 (extraction), D8080 (comprehensive ortho adolescent), D3310 (anterior root canal), D4341 (periodontal scaling/root planing per quadrant), D6010 (implant). Taxonomy 1223G0001X (general), 1223X0400X (ortho), 1223S0112X (oral surgery), 1223E0200X (endo), 1223P0221X (pediatric), 1223P0300X (perio).
(b) Oral disease near-universal; pediatric-dentist supply up 121% since 2001 (ADA HPI).
(c) **202,485 professionally active dentists (2024); 59.5 per 100,000**, ranging 40.2 (Arkansas) to 103.2 (DC). 21.2% in a recognized specialty. DSO affiliation 16.1% (2024). ~72 dental schools (ADA HPI).
(c2) Nearly 60M Americans live in dental shortage areas (HRSA, March 2024; geographic threshold 5,000:1, 4,000:1 high-need). Rural dentist supply declining.
(d) Chair utilization, hygiene recall rate, case acceptance, production per operatory.
(e) Largely commercial dental insurance + cash; Medicaid adult dental varies widely by state; limited Medicare dental.
(f) ADA Health Policy Institute; HRSA; CMS dental claim guidance.

### FACILITY & SITE TYPES

#### Ambulatory Surgery Centers (ASCs)

(a) ASC facility claims; CPT surgical codes on the ASC Covered Procedures List; APC-based ASC payment; POS 24. Taxonomy 261QA1903X.
(b) Per ASC Data (Feb 2025), "more than 80% of surgeries are now performed in an outpatient setting"; ASCs concentrate in GI, ophthalmology, orthopedics, pain, increasingly cardiology.
(c) **6,394–6,504 Medicare-certified ASCs (ASCA/CMS, Sept 2024–Q2 2025); ~12,294 total ASCs including non-certified (ASC Data, Q2 2025)**; 18,000+ ORs; avg 2.89 ORs and 2.84 specialties per Medicare-certified ASC. California leads with 894 (Sept 2024).
(c2) Maryland highest density (36 ASCs per 100,000 Part B beneficiaries, MedPAC); Kentucky, WV, Vermont ≤4. Low ASC concentration in rural/high-social-risk areas limits the HOPD-to-ASC shift (MedPAC Ch.10).
(d) Clean-claim ~98%, denial <5% (industry); case throughput, on-time starts, OR turnover, SSI rates. Per a UnitedHealth Group 2020 analysis, shifting procedures to ASCs would save consumers an average of $684 per procedure (HOPD average $7,716 in 2019, ~144% higher than ASC).
(e) Medicare ASC payment system (APC-linked subset of OPPS); commercial-heavy payer mix.
(f) CMS Provider of Services file; ASCA; MedPAC Ch.10; Definitive Healthcare.

#### Acute Care & Critical Access Hospitals

(a) UB-04; MS-DRGs (inpatient IPPS); APCs (outpatient OPPS); revenue codes 010x (room/board), 0450 (ED), 0360 (OR). POS 21 (inpatient), 22 (on-campus outpatient), 23 (ED). Taxonomy 282N00000X (general acute), 282NC0060X (CAH).
(b) Hospitals treat the full acuity spectrum; the ED is a key access point.
(c) **~6,093–6,120 total US hospitals; ~916,752 staffed beds (AHA, FY2022/2024 surveys)**; ~1,165 critical access hospitals reporting in 2024 (Definitive Healthcare). 68% of hospitals are system-affiliated.
(c2) CAHs are ≤25 beds, generally >35 miles from another hospital, serving rural areas as often the only option; rural closures are raising access concerns.
(d) Occupancy, ALOS, 30-day readmissions (HRRP), HAIs (CLABSI/CAUTI/SSI), HCAHPS, ED wait/boarding, condition mortality (HF/AMI/pneumonia).
(e) IPPS (MS-DRG) inpatient; OPPS (APC) outpatient; Medicare/Medicaid/commercial mix; CAHs paid 101% of reasonable cost.
(f) AHA Hospital Statistics; CMS Care Compare; MedPAC.

#### Behavioral Health Facilities (Inpatient Psych, Residential, SUD, Autism/ABA)

(a) Revenue codes 0114/0124/0134 (psych room/board), 0900-series (behavioral health treatment); CPT 90791/90792; HCPCS H-codes for SUD/rehab (e.g., H0015 intensive outpatient, H0010 detox); ABA CPT 97151–97158; ICD-10 F-codes, F84.0 (autism). IPF-PPS per diem; MS-DRG 880–887. Taxonomy 283Q00000X (psych hospital), 324500000X (SUD).
(b) **61.5M adults with AMI (23.4%); 14.6M with SMI (5.6%); 48.4M people 12+ with SUD (2024 NSDUH).** Of the 52.6M who needed substance-use treatment in 2024, only 3.5% (~10.2M) received it — ~80% untreated. Autism: **1 in 31 (3.2%) of 8-year-olds (CDC ADDM 2022, released April 2025)**, up from 1 in 36 (2020).
(c) **27,957 SU+MH facilities known to SAMHSA in 2024; ~15,421 MH-treatment facilities (≈12,015 outpatient, 2,460 residential, 1,288 hospital inpatient — see caveat); ~17,829 SU-treatment facilities; 2,151 SAMHSA-certified opioid treatment programs (May 2024)** (SAMHSA N-SUMHSS 2024).
(c2) Mental-health HPSAs are widespread; severe rural shortages.
(d) 30-day psychiatric readmission, FUH (HEDIS), restraint/seclusion rates, ABA treatment-hour authorizations.
(e) IPF-PPS (inpatient psych); Medicaid/commercial for residential/SUD; OTPs paid a bundled weekly Medicare rate (HCPCS G-codes).
(f) SAMHSA NSDUH & N-SUMHSS; CDC ADDM; CMS IPF-PPS.

#### Skilled Nursing Facilities (SNF)

(a) UB-04; PDPM HIPPS code on revenue code 0022; MDS 3.0 assessment-driven; ICD-10 primary diagnosis maps to PDPM clinical category. Taxonomy 314000000X. POS 31/32.
(b) ~1.2M residents; post-acute (Medicare) + long-stay (Medicaid) populations.
(c) **~14,742 certified nursing facilities (2025, Statista/CMS); some sources cite ~16,700 SNFs**; ~1.6M licensed beds; avg ~116 beds. Texas leads (1,313), then California (1,240).
(c2) For closed metro SNFs, median travel to next SNF/swing bed <2 miles; access tighter in some counties (MedPAC Ch.6).
(d) **Occupancy ~77% (2024), recovered from 67% (2021); pre-pandemic ~80–90%**; 30-day rehospitalization, RN staffing hours/resident-day, antipsychotic use, fall rates, Five-Star rating; Medicare ALOS ~20–26 days.
(e) SNF PDPM (Medicare Part A, per-diem case-mix); Medicaid for long-stay.
(f) CMS Nursing Home Care Compare/Provider Information; MedPAC Ch.6; QCOR.

#### Home Health Agencies

(a) OASIS assessment; PDGM 30-day periods; HIPPS codes; revenue codes 042x (PT), 055x (skilled nursing); HCPCS G-codes for visits. Taxonomy 251E00000X. POS 12.
(b) Post-acute and chronic-care population, largely 65+.
(c) ~11,000+ Medicare-certified HHAs; the count declined ~1% nationally in 2024 (LA County growth raised program-integrity concerns; California agencies nearly doubled 2019–2024). Medicare spent ~$15.7B on HHAs in 2024 (MedPAC).
(d) Acute-care hospitalization rate, timely initiation of care, improvement in ambulation/self-care (OASIS-based), HHCAHPS; Medicare FFS margin ~21.2% (2024, MedPAC).
(e) Home Health PDGM (Patient-Driven Groupings Model).
(f) CMS Home Health Care Compare; MedPAC; OASIS.

#### Hospice

(a) Per-diem revenue codes 0651 (routine home care), 0652 (continuous), 0655 (inpatient respite), 0656 (general inpatient); HCPCS Q5001–Q5010 (site of service); ICD-10 principal terminal diagnosis. Taxonomy 251G00000X.
(b) **Over 6,700 hospice agencies served 1.8M beneficiaries across 148M hospice days in 2024 (MedPAC)**; share of decedents using hospice reached 52.9% (2024); avg 3.9 visits/week.
(c) For-profit agencies accounted for all net provider growth; ~2.6% provider growth 2023–2024.
(d) CAHPS Hospice (8 domains), visits in last days of life, live-discharge rate; FFS margin ~8% (2023), projected 9% (2026).
(e) Medicare Hospice Benefit (four levels of care, per diem); Medicare >$28B payments in 2024.
(f) MedPAC Ch.9; CMS; NHPCO.

#### Inpatient Rehabilitation Facilities (IRF) & LTACHs

(a) **IRF:** IRF-PAI assessment; CMG (case-mix groups) under IRF-PPS; "60% rule" compliant conditions (stroke, SCI, brain injury, amputation, etc.); ICD-10 etiologic + impairment codes. Taxonomy 283X00000X. **LTACH:** LTCH-PPS (MS-LTC-DRGs); average length of stay >25 days by statutory definition. Taxonomy 282E00000X.
(b) IRF requires ability to tolerate 3 hours/day intensive therapy; LTACH serves prolonged-acuity patients (vent weaning, complex wounds).
(c) IRFs = freestanding rehab hospitals + hospital rehab units; Medicare spent ~$11B on IRFs in 2024 (MedPAC). IRF improper-payment rate 26.5% (2024, CMS).
(d) Functional improvement (self-care/mobility GG-items), discharge-to-community rate; rehabilitation physician must conduct face-to-face visits ≥3 days/week.
(e) IRF-PPS (per discharge, CMG-weighted); LTCH-PPS.
(f) CMS IRF/LTCH Compare; IRF-PAI; MedPAC.

#### Urgent Care & Retail/Convenient Care Clinics

(a) E/M 99202–99215; "S" codes S9083/S9088 (some payers' global urgent-care fee); POS 20 (urgent care), 17 (walk-in retail). Taxonomy 261QU0200X (urgent care), 261QC1500X. ICD-10 J06.9, J02.9 (strep), S-codes (minor injury), N39.0 (UTI).
(b) In 2024, 26% of adults visited an urgent care and 19.0% a retail clinic in the past 12 months (CDC NCHS Data Brief 562, 2024). One-quarter of UC patients lack a primary-care home.
(c) **~11,877 active urgent care centers (Definitive Healthcare, June 2025); industry sources cite ~14,000–15,000 (Urgent Care Association/JUCM)**; retail clinics contracting (Walmart exited; Walgreens/VillageMD closures).
(c2) The vast majority of Americans have an urgent care within a short drive (UCA, using RUCA codes); ~97% of UC encounters take ≤1 hour, 92% seen ≤30 min.
(d) Door-to-provider ≤30 min; 98% of UC patients in the appropriate setting (only 2% referred to ED); antibiotic stewardship a focus.
(e) Commercial 55%, Medicare/Medicaid 22%, cash 10%, occ-health/workers'-comp 7% (industry); PFS-based.
(f) CDC NCHS NHIS; UCA; Definitive Healthcare.

#### FQHCs & Rural Health Clinics

(a) FQHC PPS per-visit encounter codes (G0466–G0470); RHC all-inclusive rate; POS 50 (FQHC), 72 (RHC). Taxonomy 261QF0400X (FQHC), 261QR1300X (RHC).
(b) **In 2024, FQHCs served >32.4M people via 139.4M visits; ~90% of patients at or below 200% FPL.** Ensured 3.6M had controlled hypertension and 2.2M controlled diabetes (HRSA/BPHC).
(c) **~1,400 HRSA-funded health center organizations operating >16,200 service sites** (HRSA/BPHC 2024); NACHC counts 1,512 CHCs at >17,000 locations with 326,000 FTEs. One in five rural residents served.
(c2) Located in shortage areas by mission; auto-designated as HPSAs.
(d) UDS clinical quality measures (hypertension/diabetes control, cancer screening, prenatal care); better outcomes at lower cost despite sicker, poorer patients.
(e) FQHC PPS (Medicare), Medicaid PPS/APM; sliding fee scale; Section 330 grants.
(f) HRSA UDS / Health Center Program data; NACHC.

#### Dialysis Centers

(a) Hemodialysis CPT 90935/90937; ESRD MCP 90951–90970; revenue code 0821; HCPCS for ESAs; ICD-10 N18.6, Z99.2. Taxonomy 261QE0700X. POS 65 (ESRD facility).
(b) **As of March 31, 2025, dialysis facilities treated 516,837 dialysis patients (433,396 in-center HD, 78,407 home, 5,034 in SNF/NH) plus 316,873 with functioning transplants** (ESRD Networks). Adjusted ESRD incidence fell 18.6% over the decade (USRDS 2024 ADR).
(c) **18 ESRD Networks serve 7,556 dialysis centers and 223 transplant centers** (ESRD Networks). Two large dialysis organizations dominate.
(c2) In-center HD requires thrice-weekly travel; rural patients face significant burden; home dialysis (PD/HHD) <16% of patients.
(d) Kt/V ≥1.2 adequacy, vascular access (AVF/AVG vs catheter), hospitalization, standardized mortality/transfusion ratios (DFC Star ratings, QIP).
(e) ESRD PPS (bundled per-treatment) + MCP; near-universal Medicare ESRD entitlement.
(f) USRDS ADR; CMS Dialysis Facility Compare/EQRS; ESRD Networks.

#### Infusion Centers

(a) Infusion CPT 96365–96368, 96413–96417; J-codes for biologics/drugs; revenue code 0260/0510. Taxonomy 261QI0500X.
(b) Serves oncology, rheumatology, GI (IBD biologics), neurology (MS), immunology; growing shift from hospital outpatient.
(c) Freestanding and office-based infusion suites expanding.
(d) Chair utilization, drug-waste minimization, reaction rates, prior-auth turnaround.
(e) Part B buy-and-bill; OPPS vs office (PFS) site differential.
(f) CMS Part B; National Infusion Center Association.

#### Imaging/Radiology Centers

(a) CT/MRI/US/mammo CPT (74177, 70553, 76700, 77067) with TC/26 split; revenue 0350 (CT), 0610 (MRI). Taxonomy 261QD0000X. POS 11/22.
(b) High-volume diagnostic backbone across specialties.
(c) RadNet is the largest freestanding operator (440 centers as of May 2026); market remains fragmented (historically ~$17B+); CDI/SimonMed among large chains.
(d) Scanner utilization, report turnaround, repeat/recall rates, ACR accreditation, radiation dose.
(e) Freestanding bills MPFS (often lower than HOPD/OPPS); strong site-of-service payment differential.
(f) ACR; Medicare Part B; Definitive Healthcare.

#### Freestanding Emergency Departments

(a) ED E/M CPT 99281–99285; revenue code 0450; POS 23 (some states). Taxonomy 261QE0002X.
(b) 24/7 emergency care with CT/X-ray/lab; suburban/underserved-urban placement.
(c) Independent FSED market ~$1.46B (2024, Grand View Research); some states (CA, NJ) require hospital affiliation; absent in others (e.g., Alaska).
(d) Door-to-provider, LWBS rate, imaging capability; individual sites serve tens of thousands of visits/year.
(e) Bill as ED (facility + professional); reimbursement and surprise-billing scrutiny.
(f) Grand View Research; state CON/licensure data.

#### Clinical & Pathology Laboratories

(a) Lab CPT 80048/80053 (metabolic panels), 85025 (CBC), 80061 (lipid), 87xxx (microbiology/molecular); anatomic path 88305. CLFS governs clinical lab; PFS for pathology professional. Taxonomy 291U00000X (clinical), 291900000X. Revenue 0300-series.
(b) Labs underpin a large share of clinical decisions; high test volumes.
(c) Two national reference labs dominate alongside hospital and physician-office labs; all CLIA-certified.
(d) Test turnaround time, critical-value reporting, specimen-rejection rate, proficiency testing.
(e) CLFS (clinical lab, PAMA-driven rate cuts); CPT/PFS for pathology.
(f) CMS CLFS; CDC CLIA; CAP.

#### Medical/Dermatology Aesthetics

(a) Largely non-covered/cash: botulinum toxin J0585 + admin 64612 (cosmetic = non-covered), dermal fillers, laser; CPT 15780-series (dermabrasion). Taxonomy under dermatology/plastics.
(b) Driven by consumer demand; not epidemiologically defined.
(d) Provider productivity, rebooking rates, product margin.
(e) Cash-pay dominant; no Medicare.
(f) ASDS/ASAPS industry surveys.

#### Audiology / Hearing

(a) Audiometry CPT 92557, tympanometry 92567, hearing aid HCPCS V5xxx; ICD-10 H90.x (hearing loss), H93.1 (tinnitus). Taxonomy 231H00000X.
(b) ~15% of US adults report hearing trouble; age-related; OTC hearing aids (since 2022) expanding access.
(c) Audiologists ~19,871 (BLS/HRSA).
(e) Diagnostic covered by Medicare (with order); hearing aids largely non-covered (cash/commercial).
(f) BLS OEWS; ASHA.

#### Durable Medical Equipment (DME)

(a) HCPCS E-codes (E0601 CPAP, E1390 oxygen concentrator), K-codes (wheelchairs), L-codes (orthotics/prosthetics); modifiers RR (rental)/NU (new). Taxonomy 332B00000X. POS 12.
(b) Serves chronic respiratory, mobility, and diabetic-supply populations.
(e) DMEPOS fee schedule; competitive-bidding history; Medicare Part B.

#### Optical / Vision

(a) Refraction 92015, eyeglasses V2020-series, contact lenses V2500-series. Taxonomy 156FX1800X (optician).
(b) Refractive error near-universal with age; vision plans common.
(e) Vision insurance + cash; limited Medicare (post-cataract glasses covered).
(f) Vision Council; BLS.

#### Physical, Occupational & Speech Therapy

(a) **PT:** evaluation CPT 97161–97163, therapeutic exercise 97110, manual therapy 97140; **OT:** 97165–97167, 97535; **SLP:** 92507, 92526 (dysphagia). ICD-10 M-codes, R47.1, R13.1 (dysphagia). Taxonomy 225100000X (PT), 225X00000X (OT), 235Z00000X (SLP).
(b) Musculoskeletal, post-surgical, neuro-rehab, and pediatric developmental populations.
(c) **Physical therapists ~267,200 jobs (BLS 2024, $101,020 median); OTs ~140,243; SLPs ~187,083** (HRSA/BLS).
(d) Visits per episode, functional outcome (FOTO), units/visit, plan-of-care adherence; Medicare therapy thresholds.
(e) PFS (8-minute rule for timed codes); KX modifier for therapy thresholds; commercial + Medicare.

#### Chiropractic

(a) CMT CPT 98940 (1–2 regions), 98941 (3–4), 98942 (5 regions); ICD-10 M99.0x (segmental dysfunction), M54.x. Taxonomy 111N00000X. POS 11.
(b) Back/neck pain very common; chiropractic as a nonsurgical/drug-free pain option.
(c) **~71,101 chiropractors (BLS/HRSA)**; employment projected +10% 2024–2034.
(e) Medicare covers only spinal manipulation (98940–98942) with AT modifier; much is cash/commercial.

#### Podiatry

(a) Nail debridement CPT 11721, callus 11055-series, bunionectomy 28296; ICD-10 L60.x, E11.621 (diabetic foot ulcer), M20.1 (hallux valgus). Taxonomy 213E00000X.
(b) Diabetic foot care central (diabetes 40.1M); routine foot care for the elderly.
(c) **~12,247 podiatrists (BLS/HRSA)**.
(e) PFS; Medicare covers medically necessary (not routine) foot care; diabetic exception.
(f) BLS OEWS; APMA.

#### Telehealth / Virtual Care & Remote Patient Monitoring

(a) E/M with POS 02 (telehealth not in home)/10 (in home) + modifier 95; RPM CPT 99453/99454 (device setup/supply), 99457/99458 (management); RTM 98975-series; audio-only 99441–99443. Taxonomy varies by rendering provider.
(b) **In 2022, 30.1% of US adults used telemedicine in the past 12 months (NCHS National Health Statistics Report No. 205), down from 37.0% (2021).** Medicare FFS: **6,749,825 telehealth users in 2024 (~25% of eligible)** (CMS Medicare Telehealth Trends Report).
(d) Visit completion/no-show, audio vs video share, latency, follow-up conversion.
(e) PFS with evolving telehealth flexibilities (originating-site rules, post-PHE extensions); RPM under PFS.
(f) CDC NCHS NHIS; CMS Telehealth Trends.

#### Specialty & Retail Pharmacy

(a) NCPDP claims (not CPT); NDC drug codes; J-codes when on the medical benefit; dispensing fees. Taxonomy 333600000X (retail), 3336S0011X (specialty).
(b) Specialty drugs (oncology, autoimmune, rare disease) a fast-growing spend; GLP-1s drove >80% of 2024 dispensing-revenue growth (Drug Channels Institute).
(c) **~88,000 total US pharmacies; 18,984 independent community pharmacies (June 2024, NCPA Digest), declining >1/day**; ~19,075 chain, 9,152 supermarket, 7,225 mass-merchant.
(c2) Pharmacy deserts emerging with closures (Rite Aid bankruptcy, Walgreens consolidation).
(d) Prescription volume (~59,644/store/year independent), generic dispensing rate ~83%, adherence (PDC), gross margin ~19.7%.
(e) PBM-administered; DIR fees; specialty via limited networks; Medicare Part D/B.
(f) NCPA Digest; Drug Channels Institute; CMS Part D.

#### Ambulance / EMS

(a) HCPCS A0425 (ground mileage), A0427 (ALS emergency), A0429 (BLS emergency), A0998 (treat-no-transport); ICD-10 by complaint; condition codes. Taxonomy 341600000X. POS 41/42.
(b) >28M 911 dispatches/year (NASEMSO 2020 National EMS Assessment).
(c) **>18,200 local 911 EMS agencies using ~73,500 ground vehicles; >750 air-medical services** (NASEMSO).
(c2) Rural response times markedly longer; air medical for remote trauma.
(d) Response-time intervals, ROSC for cardiac arrest, on-scene time, transport vs treat-in-place.

#### Veterinary (comparison vertical)

(a) No CPT/ICD-10/Medicare; proprietary practice-management codes (AVMA/VMG); no NPI/NPPES taxonomy. Cash/pet-insurance.
(b) Companion-animal demand high; majority of US households own pets.
(c) Veterinarians a distinct BLS occupation; rapid corporatization of practices.
(d) Visits/DVM/day, ARPU, rebooking — a useful, fully-retail contrast to human healthcare's payer complexity.
(e) Out-of-pocket + pet insurance; no government payer.

## Recommendations

1. **Key the reference layer on three join fields per vertical:** NUCC provider taxonomy code (links to NPPES/NPI), governing payment system (PFS/OPPS/ASC/IPPS/IRF-PPS/LTCH-PPS/PDGM/PDPM/ESRD PPS/IPF-PPS/CLFS/CDT), and primary CMS dataset. This makes each vertical queryable and keeps RCM logic aligned to the correct fee schedule.
2. **Stage data ingestion by source reliability:** Tier 1 (government primary — CMS Provider of Services file, HRSA UDS/HPSA, USRDS, SEER, CDC NCHS, SAMHSA, AAMC, BLS OEWS, MedPAC) for canonical counts; Tier 2 (society registries — ACC NCDR, AJRR, GIQuIC, AAO IRIS, SART, ADA HPI) for clinical benchmarks; Tier 3 (commercial aggregators — Definitive Healthcare, IBISWorld, market-research firms) only for facility counts lacking a government census, clearly flagged.
3. **Treat facility counts as ranges, not points.** ASC counts legitimately differ (~6,400 Medicare-certified vs ~12,294 total); urgent care ~11,877 (Definitive) vs ~14,000–15,000 (industry); SNFs ~14,742 vs ~16,700. Store source and definition with each number.
4. **Refresh cadence:** annual for USRDS ADR, SEER/ACS, AAMC dashboard, AHA Hospital Statistics, ADA HPI, NSDUH, MedPAC March/June reports, and the CMS annual rulemaking cycle (IPPS/OPPS/PFS/SNF-PPS/IRF-PPS/HH-PPS/ESRD-PPS/hospice/IPF final rules); quarterly for HRSA HPSA designations.
5. **Benchmarks that should trigger re-derivation:** if a vertical's facility count, occupancy/utilization, or shortage-area designation crosses a materiality threshold (e.g., SNF occupancy back above 80%, urgent-care count exceeding the next industry tier, HPSA population shifts), re-derive the access and capacity layers.

## Caveats

- **Code lists are representative, not exhaustive.** High-frequency CPT/HCPCS/ICD-10/CDT/DRG/APC/taxonomy codes are illustrative of each vertical's day-to-day activity; actual claim adjudication requires current code sets (CPT updated annually by AMA, ICD-10-CM annually by CMS/NCHS, CDT by ADA, MS-DRG/APC by CMS). Always validate against the current-year release before billing or rate-setting.
- **Source vintage varies.** AHA hospital data reflect FY2022–2024 surveys; AAMC physician counts are 2024; USRDS reflects claims through 2022–2023; SEER/ACS 2024 estimates are modeled projections; NSDUH is 2024; several facility counts are 2024–2025. Several "2024/2025" market figures from commercial aggregators (Definitive Healthcare, IBISWorld, ResearchAndMarkets, Grand View Research) are estimates that differ from government censuses — flagged in text.
- **Projections vs realized data:** statements such as "33% of cardiology procedures in ASCs by 2025," ASC market growth to $55.3B, and physician-shortage forecasts (10,000–19,900 surgeons by 2036) are projections, not realized counts, and are labeled as such.
- **Telehealth adult utilization:** the most recent NCHS/NHIS published adult figure is 30.1% (2022); no later NHIS adult telehealth brief was located, so 2024 adult utilization is not yet authoritatively published (CMS Medicare-specific 2024 figures are available and cited).
- **Behavioral-health facility setting breakdown** (1,288 inpatient / 2,460 residential MH) traces to N-SUMHSS 2024 data via a secondary citation; confirm against the official N-SUMHSS 2024 Annual Detailed Tables for the SU-residential subcount if precision is required.
- **Distance/access figures** (8.0 miles/17.3 min to ED, BMC Health Services Research 2022; 10.5 miles rural to hospital, Pew 2018) are national averages that mask large local variation, especially rural; the Pew analysis is now several years old.
- **Diabetes prevalence** is cited at 40.1M/12.0% (CDC National Diabetes Statistics Report, 2023 data); an earlier CDC vintage reported 38.4M/11.6% for 2021 — use the most recent CDC release.
- No figures in this report should be used for billing, payment, or contracting without verification against the current CMS fee schedules and code sets.
