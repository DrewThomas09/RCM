# PE Deal Structuring (concepts)

How LBO deals are structured and priced — the nuanced terms that move value.
General PE definitions; for PEdesk's deal math see the bridge / returns cards.

## LBO basics
- A leveraged buyout funds the purchase with a mix of equity + debt; debt
  amplifies equity returns and risk. Sources & uses = where the money comes
  from and what it pays for at close.
- Entry/exit multiple (EV/EBITDA): the single biggest controllable return
  lever. Underwriting flat-to-down exit vs entry is the disciplined assumption.
- Net debt = gross debt − cash; equity value = enterprise value − net debt.

## Debt & covenants
- Leverage = net debt / EBITDA. Covenants (max leverage, min DSCR/FCCR) set the
  guardrails; covenant headroom is the distance to a breach. Interest coverage
  = EBITDA / interest; DSCR/FCCR also capture principal (and leases for FCCR).

## Purchase-price mechanics
- QoE (Quality of Earnings): scrubs reported EBITDA to a defensible adjusted
  figure via add-backs (one-time, owner, run-rate) — directly moves the price.
- Working-capital peg: a normalized target NWC the buyer must be left with;
  deviations adjust the price at close. Net-debt adjustment trues up cash/debt.
- Earnout: deferred, performance-contingent consideration that bridges a
  valuation gap (and creates alignment + accounting risk). Escrow / holdback:
  funds set aside to cover post-close claims (e.g. indemnities).

## Risk allocation
- Reps & warranties allocate risk on the business's condition; RWI (R&W
  insurance) shifts that risk to an insurer. MAC (material adverse change) lets
  a buyer walk if something major breaks before close.
- Rollover equity: sellers/management re-invest into the new entity (alignment).
  Management incentive plan (MIP): equity pool to motivate the team.
