# PE Value Creation & Exit (concepts)

How PE firms create and realize value after buying a company. General PE
definitions; for PEdesk's per-deal value bridge see the bridge card.

## Value-creation levers
Returns decompose into: EBITDA growth (organic + M&A), margin/cost improvement,
multiple expansion, and deleveraging.
- Organic growth: volume, pricing, new sites (de novo), share gains.
- M&A / roll-up: buying add-ons, often below the platform's multiple, so the
  blended entry multiple falls (multiple arbitrage); plus cost/revenue
  synergies. Platform = the anchor company; add-ons bolt onto it.
- Operational: cost-out (procurement, labor, footprint), pricing/payer-contract
  optimization, working-capital release (faster cash collection).
- The 100-day plan / value-creation plan sequences these into owned
  initiatives with targets — tracked actual-vs-plan.

## Multiple expansion vs growth
- "Did returns come from paying-up (multiple expansion) or from growing the
  business (EBITDA growth)?" Underwriting that relies on multiple expansion is
  riskier than underwriting EBITDA growth.

## Exit routes
- Strategic sale (to an operating acquirer — often the highest price via
  synergies), sponsor-to-sponsor / secondary (sale to another PE fund), IPO
  (public listing), continuation vehicle (GP moves a winner into a new fund to
  hold longer — extends hold but draws conflict scrutiny), dividend recap
  (lever up to return cash to LPs without selling).
- Hold period is a first-order IRR driver; the right buyer at the right time
  can out-matter the entry price. Exit readiness = how prepared the asset is to
  go to market (financials, management, growth story, clean diligence).
