# PE Fund Economics & Returns (concepts)

Standard private-equity fund mechanics, so the Guide can explain nuanced terms
an analyst uses. These are general PE definitions (not a specific PEdesk
computation); for PEdesk's own figures see the metric cards.

## Capital & the J-curve
- Committed capital = what LPs pledged; called/paid-in = drawn so far; dry
  powder = committed but uncalled. Capital is called over the investment
  period as deals close.
- J-curve: early in a fund, fees + early markdowns push net returns negative
  before exits turn them positive — a low DPI on a young fund is normal, on an
  old fund is a concern.

## Performance multiples
- MOIC = total value / invested equity (gross vs net of fees/carry differ —
  always confirm which). IRR = time-weighted annualized return; sensitive to
  timing and flattered by early distributions.
- TVPI = (distributions + residual NAV) / paid-in; DPI = distributions /
  paid-in (realized cash); RVPI = residual NAV / paid-in (unrealized).
  DPI + RVPI = TVPI.

## GP / LP economics
- Management fee: ~1.5–2% of committed (then invested) capital — funds
  operations, not returns.
- Carried interest (carry): the GP's ~20% share of profits ABOVE a hurdle
  (preferred return, often ~8%). The hurdle protects LPs' minimum return first.
- Waterfall: order of cash distribution — return of capital → preferred return
  → GP catch-up → carry split. "European" (whole-fund) waterfall pays carry
  only after the whole fund clears its hurdle; "American" (deal-by-deal) pays
  carry per deal (more GP-favorable).
- GP commitment: the GP's own capital in the fund (alignment). Vintage = the
  fund's first-investment year (a major, largely-exogenous return driver).
- Recycling: reinvesting early proceeds to deploy more than committed capital.

## Reading nuance
- A high TVPI driven by RVPI (unrealized marks) is softer than the same TVPI
  driven by DPI (realized cash). Net (post-fee/carry) figures are what LPs earn.
