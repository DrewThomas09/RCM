# Healthcare-PE Nuances (concepts)

Healthcare-specific diligence concepts a PE analyst must reason about. General
domain definitions; for PEdesk's computed figures see the metric/data cards.

## Reimbursement & payer mix
- Payer mix (Medicare / Medicaid / commercial / self-pay) drives margin because
  payers reimburse very differently: commercial > Medicare > Medicaid (often
  below cost). High Medicare exposure = inflation/rate-update risk; high
  Medicaid = below-cost risk; commercial concentration = contract-renewal risk.
- Site-of-service shift (inpatient → outpatient / ASC / home) changes
  reimbursement and economics.

## Value-based & risk
- Fee-for-service vs value-based care (VBC): VBC ties payment to outcomes/cost
  (ACOs, shared savings, capitation). Risk adjustment (RAF) scales Medicare
  Advantage revenue to patient acuity — coding accuracy matters and carries
  audit risk. MA Star ratings drive bonus payments.

## Revenue cycle (RCM)
- RCM = getting paid: charge capture → clean claim → adjudication → collection.
  Key leaks: initial-denial rate, underpayments, days-in-AR, bad debt. RCM
  improvement is a concrete, near-term EBITDA lever in provider deals.

## Regulatory & compliance
- Stark Law (physician self-referral) and Anti-Kickback Statute constrain
  referral economics — structuring must respect them. CON (certificate of
  need) gates new capacity in some states. 340B is a drug-discount program that
  changes pharmacy margin for eligible providers. HIPAA governs data.

## Operational risk
- Provider (physician) retention is a first-order risk — losing providers loses
  revenue. Labor/locum cost is a fast-moving margin driver. Quality scores
  (CMS star ratings, readmissions) increasingly tie to reimbursement and exit
  attractiveness.
