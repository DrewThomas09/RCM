# Business Model & Monetization Plan

The platform sits at the intersection of three established budget
lines: **PE diligence software** (Bloomberg / CapIQ / PitchBook
territory — per-seat $15-30K/yr), **healthcare data tools**
(Trilliant / Definitive / Komodo — $25-100K/yr feeds), and
**deal-management platforms** (DealCloud / Intralinks — per-deal
or per-firm). Each of those models maps cleanly onto a different
buyer behavior. This document maps the path from technology to
sustainable revenue.

## Buyer landscape

| Segment | AUM | Team size | Pain point | Budget mechanic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Boutique healthcare PE** | $0.3-3B | 3-12 ppl | Outbid by larger firms because of slower diligence | Annual budget; <$200K total for tooling |
| **Mid-market healthcare PE** | $3-15B | 15-40 ppl | Coordinating multi-person diligence; defending IC | Per-fund or annual ($500K-2M tooling budget) |
| **Mega-fund healthcare verticals** | $50B+ AUM | 40-150 ppl | Compliance + audit + data lineage; custom analytics | Enterprise procurement; $2-10M tooling line |
| **Healthcare-focused FoF / family office** | $0.1-1B | 2-8 ppl | Sourcing + screening; less diligence depth needed | Project-based or per-deal |
| **Healthcare strategics** (M&A teams at HCA, UnitedHealth) | n/a | 20-100 ppl | Adjacent acquisitions; integration-aware diligence | Annual; aligned with corp dev budget |
| **Sell-side advisors** (Lazard / Houlihan / Triple Tree) | n/a | 10-50 ppl per healthcare team | Building CIMs + bidder management | Per-mandate; embedded in deal fees |

**Primary go-to-market: mid-market healthcare PE** — biggest pain
point, clearest budget mechanic, fastest sales cycle, most aligned
with current platform feature set.

**Adjacent expansions** (boutique below; mega-fund above) wait for
product-market-fit signal in the primary segment.

## Pricing model: hybrid base + per-deal usage

Three pricing mechanics in widespread use; each has tradeoffs:

| Model | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-seat** ($15-30K/yr each) | Predictable revenue; familiar PE buyer mechanic | Doesn't capture platform value when one analyst processes 50 deals; hard to price ASCs vs hospitals |
| **Per-deal** ($5-25K per deal) | Aligns price to value; partner happy when usage is low | Unpredictable cash flow for vendor; encourages deal-side cherry-picking |
| **Per-fund flat** ($300K-1M/yr) | Simple procurement; all-you-can-eat | Mega-funds underpay vs SMBs; no incentive to scale to more users |

**Recommendation: hybrid base + usage cap.**

- **Base subscription**: $30K/seat/yr × team size, billed annually.
  Covers unlimited platform access including analysis, dashboards,
  collaboration, exports, public data, basic predictors.
- **Usage rider**: One free deal-analysis per seat-year baked in.
  Above that: $5K per deep-analysis-packet (a packet covering all
  9 sections from the deal_profile_v2 — entity, market, comps,
  metrics, predictions, bridge, scenarios, risks, actions).
  Caps at $200K/yr total for the rider.

For a 10-person mid-market shop closing 8 deals/year and screening
50: ~$300K base + ~50 packets × $5K = ~$550K. Caps at $500K total.

**Why this works**: Predictable base lets the customer budget;
usage rider lets them scale up in a hot deal flow year without
re-procurement; cap protects them from runaway bills. Vendor
revenue tracks customer success (more deals = more revenue,
both win).

## Three-tier feature ladder

### Free tier — Discovery

For boutique shops + students + sell-side analysts who want to
evaluate the platform. Cap at small usage; no support; no
collaboration.

- 5 deal screenings / month
- 1 saved deal analysis at a time
- Public data (HCRIS, Census, CDC PLACES, Hospital Compare)
- Single-user only; no team features
- HTML export only; no Excel / Word / PDF
- Watermarked outputs ('Generated by SeekingChartis Free')
- Community Slack only — no SLA

### Pro tier — Working analyst ($30K/seat/yr)

The bread-and-butter tier. Mid-market PE base subscription.

- Unlimited deal screenings + saved analyses
- All public data + premium feeds (TiC payer MRFs, Hospital MRFs,
  State APCDs, AHRQ HCUP, CMS MA enrollment + Stars + benchmarks)
- All ML predictors (denial / DSO / collection / forward distress /
  improvement potential)
- Full UI scaffolding (dashboard_v3, deal profile, comparisons,
  power_table, power_chart, search, theme, breadcrumbs, keyboard
  shortcuts, etc.)
- Multi-user collaboration (deal teams, threaded comments,
  @mentions, notifications)
- Excel / Word / PDF / Markdown exports
- 1 free deep-analysis packet per seat-year; $5K per additional
- 99.5% uptime SLA, business-hours support, Slack + email

### Enterprise tier — Full platform ($150K+/yr base, custom)

For mega-funds + strategics + larger PE shops with security,
compliance, and customization needs.

- Everything in Pro
- **SSO** (SAML / OIDC) — Okta, Azure AD, Auth0
- **Custom predictors** — model retraining on the customer's
  realized exits + portfolio data
- **Audit + compliance package** — SOC 2 Type II evidence,
  HIPAA BAA, immutable audit log retention 7 years
- **Premium data sources** — Truven / IQVIA pass-through (customer's
  license required), state-by-state Medicaid managed care contracts
- **White-glove onboarding** — dedicated CSM, custom data import,
  initial deal-flow loaded by our team
- **On-premise / VPC deployment** — for shops with strict data
  residency requirements
- **API access** — programmatic access for portfolio-monitoring
  automations
- **24/7 support + 99.9% SLA**

Enterprise pricing: $150K base + per-seat above 10 + premium-data
pass-through. A 50-seat mega-fund deal would be $150K base + 40
seats × $20K/seat (volume discount) + ~$200K data = ~$1.15M/year.

### Free → Pro → Enterprise migration paths

- **Free → Pro**: When usage exceeds free-tier caps. Self-service
  upgrade in-app with annual billing. Conversion target: 8% of
  free users in 6 months.
- **Pro → Enterprise**: Triggered by customer asking for SSO,
  on-premise, or custom predictors. Sales-assisted; CSM-driven.
  Conversion target: 25% of Pro accounts within 18 months.

## What stays free forever

These features stay free across all tiers — they're competitive
defenses, not differentiators:

- Public CMS / Census / CDC data ingestion (we don't gate public
  data; that would be a hostile move)
- Read-only access to deal profiles you have a link to (public
  share URLs)
- Basic visualization (power_chart at low resolution)
- Open-source npm-style integrations / API spec (so partners can
  build plugins)

The competitive moat is the **trained predictors + ML pipelines +
analyst workflows**, not the underlying public data. Gating public
data would invite a free competitor that re-ingests the same
sources and undercuts us.

## What's premium-only

These features are Pro+ exclusive — the value the customer pays for:

- **Trained ML predictors** with the platform's calibration runs
- **Premium data feeds** (TiC payer MRFs, APCDs, paid feeds when
  customer doesn't have their own license)
- **Multi-user collaboration** — comments, @mentions, presence
- **Excel / Word / PDF exports** — not just HTML
- **Custom dashboards** — pinned widgets, default views,
  notification preferences
- **/api/global-search + advanced search** — free tier gets browse-
  only; paid tier gets full search
- **Volume-trend forecasting**, **regime detection**, **ensemble
  methods** — Pro features
- **Improvement-potential 3-scenario sweep** — Pro feature

## Adjacent monetization

Beyond the core seat-based subscription:

### Per-deal banker pass-through

Sell-side advisors building CIMs could license per-mandate access
($5-15K per deal) to use the platform's analysis as part of their
banker package. Different procurement; faster cycle than buying
seats.

### Data marketplace

Platform users opting into anonymous benchmarking get free Pro
seats; their (deal-level, not company-level) benchmarks improve
the platform's predictors. Network effect.

### Custom-research add-ons

Bespoke analytics for $25-100K per project (e.g., 'analyze every
ASC roll-up since 2018 and predict the next 18 months of
consolidation'). Higher-margin; expandable into a research-as-a-
service line.

### White-label embedded

Partner banks (smaller boutique advisors) embed the platform's
analysis engine in their own deal rooms ($50-200K/yr per advisor).
Platform stays white-label; advisor wins client.

## Revenue projection (years 1-3)

Assumptions:
- **Year 1**: 8 mid-market PE customers, 4 enterprise deals, no boutique tier yet
- **Year 2**: 25 mid-market, 10 enterprise, 50 boutique
- **Year 3**: 60 mid-market, 25 enterprise, 200 boutique

| Year | Pro ARR | Enterprise ARR | Free conversion | Total ARR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Y1 | 8 × $400K = $3.2M | 4 × $700K = $2.8M | $0 | **$6.0M** |
| Y2 | 25 × $450K = $11.3M | 10 × $850K = $8.5M | 50 × $30K free conv | $21.3M |
| Y3 | 60 × $500K = $30M | 25 × $1M = $25M | 200 free + 25% conv | **~$58M** |

These are rough. Real numbers depend on (a) mid-market CAC, (b)
willingness to pay above the existing PitchBook+CapIQ stack, and
(c) competitive entry from incumbent diligence platforms. Cohort-
expansion (existing customer adding seats) is the highest-leverage
metric to track.

## Pricing experiments to run (quarter-by-quarter)

The pricing recommendations above are starting points. Run real
experiments to refine:

- **Q1**: Land 2-3 design partners at heavily-discounted Pro tier
  ($150K) in exchange for case studies + product feedback.
- **Q2**: Test list-price Pro with 5-10 customers. Measure win
  rates vs CapIQ in head-to-head sales cycles.
- **Q3**: Experiment with usage rider tiers — does $5K/deal feel
  too expensive or too cheap? A/B test $3K vs $5K vs $7.5K.
- **Q4**: Annual contract negotiations come due. Measure churn vs
  renewal-with-expansion as the cohort signal.

## Open questions

These don't have a clear answer yet and need market data to
resolve:

1. **Will customers pay an annual subscription for what's
   ostensibly an analytics platform vs paying per-mandate?** PE
   tooling has historically been per-seat; analytics has been
   per-feed.
2. **How much of premium-data pass-through can we capture?** The
   underlying licenses (TiC / IQVIA) are expensive — does the
   customer pay direct or through us? Direct procurement is more
   common but loses revenue.
3. **What's the Enterprise SSO + audit feature timeline?**
   Enterprise contracts won't close without SSO; SSO is real
   engineering work (4-8 weeks) currently undone.
4. **What's the right discounting policy?** Mid-market shops
   negotiate 15-25% off list; mega-funds negotiate 30-40%. Set
   the floor early or risk price erosion.
5. **Is there a vertical-specific ASC / behavioral / post-acute
   pricing premium?** A specialty-only platform might command
   higher per-seat pricing than a general healthcare-PE platform.

**Near-term (next 6 months)**:

   market-fit signal.

**Medium-term (6-18 months)**:

2. Hire first sales rep for mid-market expansion.
3. Build Enterprise SSO + audit log retention.
4. Run pricing experiments per the Q1-Q4 sequence above.

**Long-term (18-36 months)**:
1. Adjacent monetization: data marketplace + per-mandate banker
   pass-through.
2. International expansion — UK / Canadian healthcare PE markets
   (data sources differ; large lift).
3. Verticalize per asset class: ASC-specialist tier, behavioral-
   health-specialist tier with premium per-asset-class pricing.

The platform is technically ready to monetize — the auth, audit,
and feature surface support all three tiers. What's missing is the
**business-side scaffolding**: pricing page, billing integration,
customer success motion, sales-rep playbook. That's the next
operational lift.
