# PEdesk Diligence Page Taxonomy — jobs-to-be-done

Every Diligence page must fit one job. Pages that don't fit are rename/move/
defer candidates.

| # | Job (question it answers) | Pages |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Provider/company financial analysis** — "how does this target's economics look vs peers?" | HCRIS X-Ray · Cost Structure · Debt Service · EBITDA Bridge · QoE Memo · Deal MC / value |
| 2 | **Revenue / payer / reimbursement** — "what's the reimbursement risk?" | Payer Stress · Payer Rate Trends · CMS APM · (deal-specific Payer Analysis) |
| 3 | **Quality / operations / clinical risk** — "what operational risk before close?" | Diligence Checklist · Root Cause · Drug Shortage / Supply Chain · Ingestion / Snapshot |
| 4 | **Competitive / regulatory / market structure** — "what's the market/reg risk?" | Antitrust Screener · Local/Contract (MA contracts) · Regulatory Calendar · Market Intel |
| 5 | **Workforce / physician / partner economics** — "is the people side sound?" | Physician Productivity · Provider Retention/Churn · Partner Economics · Management Comp |
| 6 | **Special-sector / thematic theses** — "does this sector thesis hold?" | Biosimilars · Dental Prediction · HCIT/SaaS · ESG/Sustainability · Insurance/Malpractice |
| 7 | **Reference/corpus (NOT active Diligence unless deal-attached)** | Sponsor Track Record · broad Payer Intelligence · Find Comps · Comparable Outcomes |

**Job-6 caution:** the special-sector theses are the weakest cluster — mostly
ILLUSTRATIVE with unclear purpose. They are the primary defer/delete pool until
a real source + a concrete "when do I use this on a deal?" exists.

**Job-7 rule:** reference/corpus pages live in **Research**; they may be
*callable from* a deal (e.g. "comps for THIS target") but should not masquerade
as the deal's own evidence.

For each page the reform adds a header declaring: **Purpose · Data universe ·
Source · Confidence · Next action** (see the data-source matrix).
